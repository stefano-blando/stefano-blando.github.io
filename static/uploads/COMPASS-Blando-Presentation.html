●Methodology · Prediction Targets

Defining "Stress": 40 targets across 5 dimensions.

Weekly Crashes

Rolling 5-day returns < {−3%, −5%, −7%}. Captures rare tail events with 2%–24% base rates.

Drawdown Init

Peak-to-trough declines > 10%. Captures the onset of structural bear markets.

Daily Drops

Returns < κ·σ. Identifies local tail events relative to current volatility regime.

Corr. Regimes

Average pairwise correlation > 90th pctl. Directly measures structural coupling.

VIX Regimes

VIX levels crossing 20, 25, or 30 thresholds. Measures implied volatility stress.

Backup · Q&A · 04
Honest limitations

What this work does not claim.

  • Single market. US equities only. Cross-asset and cross-region replication is open work.
  • Network ≠ orthogonal to VIX. r = 0.53–0.61. Incremental value concentrates on shock-type targets.
  • Crash labels are sparse. 40 targets help, but base rates are low; we mitigate via SMOTE + class weights.
  • Backtest, not live. Slippage modeled at 5 bps; turnover-aware. No regime-change in costs.
