Today, **Monday, May 18th, 2026**, the workshop **"COMPASS: Complexity, Markets, Policy, and AI in Social Systems"** took place at **Scuola Superiore Sant'Anna** in Pisa.

The event brought together international experts to explore how AI is reshaping our understanding of complex social and economic phenomena. Highlights included keynote lectures by **Prof. Guido Germano** (UCL), **Prof. Prabhani Kuruppumullage Don** (Penn State), and **Dr. Federica Casarosa** (SSSA).

I had the pleasure of co-organizing the workshop and participating in the panel on **AI for Complexity and Economic Systems**, discussing the potential of agent-based modeling and network science in forecasting and policy-making.

A big thank you to all participants, the co-organizers, and the **Scuola Superiore Sant'Anna** for hosting us. We also gratefully acknowledge the **University of Pisa (UniPi)** for the grant for high-impact doctoral scientific initiatives that supported this event.

This workshop represents a significant step in building an interdisciplinary bridge between AI and the social sciences.
