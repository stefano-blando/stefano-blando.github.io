On **Monday, May 18th, 2026**, the workshop **"COMPASS: Complexity, Markets, Policy, and AI in Social Systems"** took place at **Scuola Superiore Sant'Anna** in Pisa.

The event brought together international scholars to discuss how artificial intelligence is reshaping our understanding of complex social and economic dynamics. Highlights included keynote addresses by **Prof. Guido Germano** (UCL), **Prof. Prabhani Kuruppumullage Don** (Penn State), and **Dr. Federica Casarosa** (SSSA).

I had the pleasure of co-organizing the workshop and contributing to the panel on **AI for Complexity and Economic Systems**, where we discussed agent-based modeling, network science, and computational policy design.

We extend our sincere thanks to all speakers, attendees, co-organizers, and **Scuola Superiore Sant'Anna** for hosting the initiative, as well as the **University of Pisa (UniPi)** for the grant supporting high-impact doctoral scientific events.

This workshop represents a significant step forward in strengthening interdisciplinary connections between artificial intelligence and the social sciences.
